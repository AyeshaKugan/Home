Crochet Stitches

There are 3 basic stitches that are used in crochet. These are the single, double, and half-double crochet stitches. They can be manipulated to create different patterns.

The Chain Stitch

The chain stitch is used to begin a crochet project.

  1. To start a chain, make a slip knot and put it onto your hook.
  2. Wrap the yarn around the hook from back to front and grab it with the hook.
  3. Pull the hooked yarn through the slip knot and onto the hook, creating one chain stitch.
  4. Repeat Steps 2 and 3 until you have the desired number of stitches. One loop should remain on the hook.

Singe Crochet Stitch

A single crochet is the most basic crochet stitch.

Step 1: Hold your hook in your right hand with a slip knot.

Step 2: Chain however many stitches you need.

Step 3: Skip the first chain stitch and put your hook into the second stitch.

Step 4: Insert hook into the center of next chain stitch. Draw yarn through the chain stitch and up onto the hook. There are now 2 loops on the hook.

Step 5: Bring yarn over hook from back to front, and draw it through both loops on hook. One loop remains on the hook, and you have just made one single crochet stitch. Repeat for all stitches.

Step 6: At the end of the row, make one chain stitch, then turn the work counter-clockwise, leaving the hook in the chain.

Step 7: Make one single crochet stitch in the first stitch and in each remaining stitch of the previous row. Be sure to work into the last stitch. Chain 1, turn.

Step 8: Repeat steps 3-7 in every chain until your piece is your desired length. Cut the yarn from the skein, leaving a long tail.

Step 9: Draw the hook straight up, bringing the yarn through the remaining loop on the hook.

Double Crochet Stitch

A double crochet is taller than a single crochet. It is made by a "Yarn-over", which is wrapping the yarn around the hook before inserting it into the chain.

Step 1: Make a chain for a foundation.

Step 2: Wrap the yarn over your hook once.

Step 3: Insert hook into the fourth chain from the hook.

Step 4: Wrap the yarn over and pull through the chain. You should have three loops on your hook.

Step 5: Yarn over and pull through the first 2 loops on the hook.

Step 6: Yarn over again and pull through the remaining 2 loops. This creates one double crochet.

Step 7: Put one double crochet in each chain until the end.

Step 8: At the end of every row, chain 3 stitches as a turning chain.

Step 9: Cut the yarn and pull the tail through the remaining loop on the hook.

Half-Double Crochet Stitch

A half-double crochet is essentially the same as a double crochet except you put the three loops all together in one stitch rather than in two separate stitches.

Step 1: Chain a foundation row.

Step 2: Wrap the yarn over your hook once.

Step 3: Insert the hook into the third chain from the hook.

Step 4: Wrap the warn over and pull through the chain. You should have three loops on your hook.

Step 5: Wrap the yarn over and pull through all three loops. Repeat steps 2 - 5 for the remainder of the chain.

Step 6: At the end of each row, chain 2 stitches as a turning chain.

Step 7: Repeat steps 2 - 6 in every chain until your piece is your desired length.

Step 8: Cut a long-tail. Bring the yarn through the remaining loop on the hook.
